# ☁️ AWS Cloud Quest: Cloud Practitioner

I recently completed **AWS Cloud Quest: Cloud Practitioner** on AWS Skill Builder — and honestly, it's one of the more fun ways to learn cloud. Instead of sitting through hours of videos, you're dropped into an interactive game where you solve real business problems using actual AWS services in a live sandbox environment. Every challenge teaches you something practical, and by the end you've built up a solid foundation in cloud computing.

---

## 🗺️ What I Learned

### Compute — Getting Things Running
Got hands-on with **Amazon EC2** for spinning up virtual servers, and dipped into **AWS Lambda** for serverless functions. Learned how to pick the right type of compute for different situations — not everything needs a full server!

### Storage — Keeping Data Safe
Worked extensively with **Amazon S3** for object storage — creating buckets, uploading files, and managing who can access what. Also got introduced to **Amazon EBS** for block storage that sticks with your EC2 instances.

### Networking — Connecting It All Together
Learned the fundamentals of **Amazon VPC** — how to set up subnets, route tables, and gateways so your resources can talk to each other (and the internet) safely. Also configured **Security Groups** and **NACLs** to control traffic in and out.

### Databases — Storing Structured Data
Explored **Amazon RDS** for managed relational databases and **Amazon DynamoDB** for NoSQL. Came away with a much clearer sense of when you'd reach for one over the other.

### Security — Keeping Things Locked Down
Dug into **AWS IAM** — users, groups, roles, and policies. Learned the golden rule of cloud security: give people *only* the access they actually need (principle of least privilege). Also got a solid understanding of the **AWS Shared Responsibility Model**, which clears up a lot of confusion about what AWS handles vs. what's on you.

### Cloud Economics — Understanding the Bill
Learned how AWS pricing works across On-Demand, Reserved, and Spot Instances, and used the **AWS Pricing Calculator** to estimate costs before deploying anything. No nasty surprises!

### Architecture — Building Things the Right Way
Got introduced to the **AWS Well-Architected Framework** and its six pillars: Operational Excellence, Security, Reliability, Performance Efficiency, Cost Optimization, and Sustainability. Also learned the difference between **Regions**, **Availability Zones**, and **Edge Locations** — and why it matters for building resilient apps.

### Monitoring — Knowing What's Going On
Touched on **Amazon CloudWatch** for tracking metrics and setting up alerts, and **AWS CloudTrail** for keeping an audit trail of everything happening in your account.

---

## 🛠️ Hands-On Stuff

Beyond just reading about services, I was actually configuring them — deploying infrastructure through the AWS Console, troubleshooting broken setups, applying security best practices, and designing solutions that balance cost and performance. It's the kind of learning that actually sticks.
